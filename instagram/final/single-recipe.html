Bread Roll

Bread rolls are a delicious snack or appetizer made with a filling encased in a bread dough and then deep-fried or baked until golden and crispy. Here's a simple recipe to make bread rolls:

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : breakfast Snacks food

instructions

step 1

Trim the edges of the bread slices and set them aside. Heat some oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

step 2

Add the chopped onions and green chilies to the pan. Sauté until the onions become translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes along with the chopped mixed vegetables. Mix everything together. Add garam masala (if desired) and mix well. Cook for a couple of minutes until the filling is well combined.

step 3

Remove the pan from heat and let the filling cool down to room temperature. Take a bread slice and dip it in water for a second, then squeeze out the excess water gently. Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center of the bread slice. Fold the edges of the bread slice over the filling and gently roll it into a cylindrical shape, sealing the edges.

step 4

Repeat the process with the remaining bread slices and filling. Heat oil in a deep pan or skillet for deep frying. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the bread rolls into the hot oil, a few at a time. Fry the rolls until they turn golden brown and crispy from all sides. Remove them from the oil and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil. Serve the bread rolls hot with ketchup, chutney, or any desired dipping sauce.

ingredients

8 slices of bread (white or whole wheat)

1 cup of boiled and mashed potatoes

½ cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, etc.), boiled and finely chopped

1 small onion, finely chopped

2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)

1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste

½ teaspoon of cumin seeds

½ teaspoon of turmeric powder

½ teaspoon of red chili powder

½ teaspoon of garam masala (optional)

Salt to taste

Oil for frying

tools

Frying Pan or Skillet

Wooden Spoon or Spatula

Tongs or Slotted Spoon

Knife and Cutting Board
